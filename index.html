The Making

Founded

The first free airline was the Wing Airlines Company, founded on November 16, 1909. It was founded with government assistance and operated airships manufactured by The Zeppelin Corporation. Its headquarters were in Frankfurt.

Founder

John Smith is the founder of Wing Airlines, a company he started in 1909with the goal of providing affordable and comfortable air travel to customers. With a background in aviation and business, John saw an opportunity to create an airline that focused on customer satisfaction and safety. Under his leadership, Wing Airlines has grown to become a major player in the industry, with a fleet of modern aircraft and a reputation for excellence.

The aircraft was equipped with the latest in-flight entertainment systems, comfortable seating, and ample legroom. It was the first of many aircraft in our growing fleet, and it helped establish Wing Airlines as a major player in the industry.

Interior

One of the earliest airline organizations, a British group called Air Transport and Travel, Ltd., acquired several Airco D.H.4a VIII single-engine planes and modified them to include an enclosed cramped space in the fuselage with room for two adventurous passengers. The pilot’s cockpit remained open. Another early British airline, Handley Page Transport, Ltd., converted wartime twin-engine bombers to haul up to 14 passengers, who lounged in comfortable wicker chairs. These slow but roomy aircraft established a tradition of ornately embellished interiors and spacious surroundings on early European airlines.

Power

From the very invention of flight at the beginning of the 20th century, military aircraft and engines generally led the way, with commercial aviation following. This was also the case in the jet age, which began with the invention of jet engines under military sponsorship in the 1930s and ’40s. However, by the late 20th century, commercial jet-engine technology had come to rival and sometimes even lead military technology in several areas of engine design. One example of an early jet engine was the one invented by Frank Whittle of England, which powered the first British jet, the Gloster E.28/39, on its first flight on May 15, 1941.
